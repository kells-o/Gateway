Things to do in New Mexico

Bandelier National Monument

Bandelier National Monument is made of rock canyon and mesa country with human presence goiong back over 11,000 years. Humankinds marks from both past and present can be seen all throughout Bandelier. The ancestral Pueblo Peoples lived in Bandelier for approximately 400 years. They created homes out of volcanic tuff and planted their crops on mesatop fields. There is over 33,000 acres for you and your family and friends to discover. Within the park you will see petroglyphs and dwellings carved into the rock cliffs. Bandelier has many trails for visitors to discover. There are short trails that form loops or longer trails consisting of steep rocky switbacks covering miles of the park. You can also camp, go boating and take guided tours here.

Things to Know

  • Automobile/ Vehicle 7 day entry is $25 for all passengers.
  • Single 7 day entry (bike, on foot, non-commercial vehicles or organized groups) is $15 per person
  • Bandelier park is fun for any age group!
  • Bandelier visiting hours in the winter are from 9AM-5PM. Visiting hours in the summer are from 9AM-6PM.

Santa Rosa Blue Hole

Santa Rosa Blue Hole is one of the most popular diving destinations in the US for both SCUBA diving and training. The Blue Hole is a clear body of water with a constant temperature of 62 degrees farenheit. There's a constant inflow of 3,000 gallons per minute, meaning the water will look different every visit! The surface of the hole has an 80 feet diameter while the bottom has a diameter of 130 feet. There is an approximete 80 feet visibilty from the surface. The Sant Rosa Blue Hole is fun for anyone looking to learn how to dive or maybe just jump in from the cliffs above!

Things to Know

  • To be able to dive you will need to purchase an $8 permit from the City Hall or you can also purchase one at the Santa Rosa Dive Center. The permit itself is good for one week.
  • Santa Rosa Blue Hole is located at 1085 Blue Hole Road, Santa Rosa, NM 88435-2535
  • This destination is good for teens and adults. Children are welcomed, but it is preferred they do'nt enter the water.

Route 66 Musical Highway

This Musical Highway is located near Tijeras and is eastbound on Route 333, which is the old Route 66 system. The highway was engineered with rumble strips to sound like "America the Beautiful" as a part of a partnership between the New Mexico Department of Transportation and the National Geographic Channel. It was installed in 2014 to encourage drivers to drive at the posted speed limit. The speed limit is posted as 45MPH and to be able to here the "America the Beautful" you will need to drive at exactly 45MPH.

Things to Know

  • The Musical Highway is located between mile markers 4 and 5 near exit 170.
  • This highway is free to travel on. So no need to pay admission or a toll price!
  • Grab your family and jump in the car because this will be a fun trip for all ages.
